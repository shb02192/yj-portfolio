📝 Publications

Thesis (in progress)

  • Exploring the effects of narrative visualization story structure on visualization interpretation and engagement
    • Abstract: Narrative visualization is a series of interactive visualizations sequenced in order to maximize storytelling potential. Although narrative visualization is being actively utilized by journalists and storytellers, there lack theoretical, empirical studies on how diverse visualization stories affect reading experience. This study characterizes narrative visualization elements through the lens of visual narratives, and investigates the mechanism of the author and reader’s interplay inside the ’visual story’. This study views level of interactivity and author guidance to be crucial determinants of narrative visualization, and evaluates how the determinants affect visualization interpretation and engagement by a 2X2 between-subject experiment. Studying unique storytelling features of narrative visualization and quantitatively measuring reading experience will provide insight to both visualization designers and authoring tool developers.

👩🏻‍💻 Research & Projects

  • Visualization project on disability representation in Korean movies    (2020.09 ~ 2021.01 (in progress))
    • Summary: This project's goal is to explore how disabled individuals are represented in Korean movies for the past 20 years. We first gathered Korean movies which depict characters with disability. Then, using face++ face detection API (https://www.faceplusplus.com/), we automatically detected and identified each character's face emotions. The project is in progress to convey our analysis of character emotion and image data via an interactive visualization website.
